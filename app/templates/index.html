卫生技术评估研讨会001期

注册与会/Enroll

会议介绍／Event introduction

为提升我国医疗卫生领域相关行业人员对卫生技术评估的认知，推动我国卫生技术评估的发展，中国卫生经济学会与英国国家卫生与社会服务优化研究院（NICE international）积极开展合作，并定于2016年9月21日在上海市举办“卫生技术评估研讨会”。本次会议将邀请中、英卫生技术评估专家，围绕国内外卫生技术评估发展及政策转化开展研讨。

时间与地点／Time & Venue

会议时间：2016年9月21日 （9月20日下午14:00报到）

会议地点：上海市海悦酒店3号楼1层宴会B厅（地址：上海市徐汇区钦江路99号）

研讨内容／Content

1. 国际卫生技术评估发展及案例介绍；

2. 中国卫生技术评估发展及案例介绍；

3. 卫生技术评估方法学介绍；

4. 卫生技术评估成果转化。

讲师简介／Lecturers introduction

国家卫生政策专家：国家卫生计生委、上海卫生计生委相关政策专家；英国专家：英国国家卫生与社会服务优化研究院国际部(NICE international)专家；国家卫生技术评估专家：国家卫生计生委卫生发展研究中心、中国疾病预防与控制中心、上海卫生发展研究中心相关专家。

Kalipso

Kalipso Chalkidou was the founding director of NICE’s international programme and is now leading the international Decision Support Initiative from Imperial College London. Her work has focused on helping governments build technical and institutional capacity for using evidence to inform health policy. She is interested in how local information, local expertise and local institutions can drive scientific and legitimate healthcare resource allocation decisions. She has been involved in the Chinese rural health reform and also in national health reform projects in Colombia, Turkey and the Middle East, working with the World Bank, PAHO, DFID and the Inter-American Development Bank as well as national governments. Between 2007 and 2008, she spent a year at the Johns Hopkins School of Public Health, as a Harkness fellow in Health Policy and Practice, studying how comparative effectiveness research can inform policy and US government drug pricing policies.

She holds a doctorate on the molecular biology of prostate cancer from the University of Newcastle (UK), an MD (Hons) from the University of Athens and is a visiting Professor at King’s College London, a senior advisor on international policy at the Center for Medical Technology Policy (USA) and visiting faculty at the Johns Hopkins Berman Institute for Bioethics.

Francis

Francis Ruiz joined the NICE International team as a senior adviser in 2010, and moved to the Imperial College London’s Institute of Global Health Innovation in September 2016 to continue as part of the core team developing the International Decision Support Initiative. He previously worked in the Guidelines and Technology Appraisal teams at NICE as a technical adviser and analyst from 2003-10. He has a specialist interest in health economics and has worked on a number of technology appraisals and guidelines in a variety of therapeutic areas. Prior to his work at NICE, Francis worked for over six years in clinical data management and health economics within the pharmaceutical sector. Francis graduated with a BSc in physiology from the University of Dundee in 1993. He also holds a post-graduate diploma in psychology from the Royal Holloway (University of London) and an MSc in Health Policy, Planning and Financing from the London School of Hygiene/London School of Economics.
